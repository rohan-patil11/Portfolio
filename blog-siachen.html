Life on Siachen Glacier

Date: May 2025

The Siachen Glacier, located at the northern tip of India, is known as the world's highest battlefield. Indian soldiers deployed here face temperatures as low as -60Â°C, avalanches, and thin oxygen levels while maintaining security at 18,000+ feet.

Soldiers undergo intense training before deployment, and logistics like ration, clothing, and medical support are flown in by helicopter. Despite extreme hardships, their morale and discipline remain unmatched.

Troops are rotated every 3 months, and the deployment is considered one of the toughest in the Indian Army. The glacier is strategically significant as it borders Pakistan and China.

Life here is not just about survival; it involves constant vigilance, teamwork, and innovation to overcome environmental challenges. Soldiers use specially designed equipment and shelters to combat frostbite and altitude sickness.

The human spirit's resilience on Siachen is a testament to the bravery and dedication of Indian soldiers who protect the nation's sovereignty in the harshest conditions on earth.
